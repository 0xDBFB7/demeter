When you click the “Run Feff” button on the FEFF tab, the FEFF calculation is run. Once finished, a succinct summary of the calculation is displayed on the Paths tab.

Some statistics about the FEFF calculation are shown in the Description text box. Below that is the summary of the paths found in the FEFF calculation. This summary is presented in the form of a table. Each row describes a unique scattering path.

The first column shows the degeneracy of the path. The second column shows its nominal path length, Reff . That is value that will be used in any math expression containing the reff token.

The third column shows a simple view of the scattering path. The @ token represents the absorber, thus appears as the first and last token in each description. The tokens representing the scattering atoms are taken from the tags on the FEFF tab.

The fourth column contains a heuristic assessment of the importance of the path. Single scattering paths and collinear multiple scattering paths are considered high importance paths. They have a value of 2 in this column and are colored with green text. Certain kinds of triangle paths are considered of intermediate importance. These have a value of 1 and are colored with yellow text. Other paths are considered to be of low importance and are colored with grey text. Don't put too much faith in this assessment of importance. You should explicitly check all paths to decide if they should be included in a fit.

The fifth column gives the number of legs in each path. The final column is a simple explanation of the shape of the scattering geometry.

The rows in this table are selectable by mouse click. Left clicking on a row selects that row. Control clicking on another row adds it to the selection. Shift clicking adds to the selection all rows between the one clicked upon and the previously clicked upon row.

Much of the functionality of this page rests upon the set of selected paths. Most importantly, selecting paths is the first step to using paths in a fitting model. This will be explained in the next chapter.

At the top of the Paths tab are a series of buttons. The “Save” button will write the entire FEFF calculation to a form that can be reimported into ARTEMIS or used in some other context in a DEMETER script. The remaining buttons are related to making plots of the selected paths.

A plot of paths from raw FEFF calculation. In this example, the first four paths from the nickel metal calculation were selected, then the “Plot selection” button was pressed. In this plot, we see that the first two single scattering paths are, indeed, quite large. The intermediate importance third path, which scatters among first coordination shell Ni atoms, is a modest contributor to the EXAFS. The low importance fourth shell path, which also scatters among first coordination shell atoms, cannot even be seen on this scale. It truly is a low importance path.

It is, therefore, very quick and easy to examine the results of a FEFF calculation. The other four buttons are used to select how the plot of paths is made. The options are χ(k) , |χ(R)| , Re[χ(R)] , and Im[χ(R)] . The k-weight selected in the Plot window is used to make the plot of paths.
